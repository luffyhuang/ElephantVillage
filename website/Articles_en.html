If we want to better protect wild animals, we should start with understanding them. Wild animals like Asian elephants are neither cruel, ferocious, powerful beasts, nor elegant, easy-going, and submissive pets.

Challenges, causes and future path of human-elephant conflict in Village DaShuJiao
"Dear elephants please leave, no destructions any-more!" said a little girl in Dashujiao Village. The Asian elephant is a National First-class Protected Animal in China. The number of Asian elephants living in China has increased from 170 in the 1980s to about 300 today. At present, they are distributed in Xishuangbanna Prefecture, Pu'er City, and Lincang City in Yunnan Province.
"Be careful! The Asian Elephant comes"
"The elephant is the ancestor of the Dai people, and we worship it very much. It was not until five years ago that they came here. It was about four in the morning, and I went with another guy to pick up some stuff. It was quite dark, and an elephant was right on the road, walking up to us with little sound. It shook our car with its nose a few times and then went away. I was so scared when I saw it that my legs were shaking. It felt like my legs were not even my own." Said a villager from a farm in Menga town.
